Act 01 — What CWM is

Collaboration inside the enterprise. That was the aim.

CWM stands for Collaborative Work Management. It's ServiceNow's product for managing everyday work inside the Now Platform — the same platform that already runs ITSM, HR, customer service, and portfolio management for 85% of the Fortune 500.

The original aim: give ServiceNow customers a place where boards, docs, and tasks live together. A simpler, friendlier layer on top of the enterprise. No more switching to Asana or Monday.com for lightweight work. Manage it here — where your enterprise data already lives.

CWM shipped fast since January 2024 — boards, docs, kanban, Gantt, sprint planning, 7 AI skills, Connected Work (pull items from any ServiceNow table), bidirectional sync with Enterprise Agile Planning (EAP), formulas, and automations. The collaboration surface is clean and usable. The question is: why would anyone come to it?

Act 03 — The problem

Boards and docs in one place isn't enough.

CWM offers a genuinely simpler collaboration experience than navigating ServiceNow's complex modules directly. But "simpler" doesn't pull people in. People don't switch to a new tool because it's easier. They switch because it does something the old way can't.

1. SPM users already live in ServiceNow — just fragmented.

An SPM user's morning isn't about choosing between tools. They're already on ServiceNow — but they're hopping between siloed modules that don't talk to each other:

Email / Slack
"What landed overnight?" — approvals, messages, escalations.
EAP
"Where's my sprint?" — stories, backlog, sprint board.
ITSM module
"What incidents need me?" — separate from sprint context.
HR portal
"Any open requests?" — completely disconnected from work boards.
PPM module
"What approvals are pending?" — no link to task-level impact.
CWM
Optional. Nothing triggers them to come here first.

All of this data is in the same platform, the same database. But the user experiences it as 5 separate apps. CWM could unify this — but today it's just another stop, not the starting point.

2. Some teams escape to outside tools entirely.

When ServiceNow's own modules feel too heavy for everyday collaboration, some SPM teams adopt Asana or Monday.com on the side. Those tools are simple and fast — but completely disconnected from ServiceNow's enterprise data. CWM was built to prevent this escape. But "boards and docs in one place" isn't a strong enough pull when Asana already does that well.

3. The unique advantage is invisible.

CWM sits on the same database as ITSM, HR, PPM, and EAP. Connected Work can already pull from any of these tables. That's a structural advantage no outside tool can replicate. But today it shows up as rows in a board column — not as intelligence. The thing that makes CWM different from Asana or Monday is the thing CWM doesn't surface.

Act 04 — The one thing nobody else has

Collaboration with enterprise context.

A board in Asana or Monday is blind. It only knows what you put on it manually. A board in CWM can see your EAP sprint, your ITSM incidents, your PPM approvals, your HR requests — because they're all in the same database. That's not an integration. That's native access.

But today CWM doesn't use that access for anything intelligent. Connected Work displays rows from other tables. It doesn't reason about them. The value isn't seeing your incident on a board. It's knowing that incident is blocking your sprint story and your sprint ends Thursday.

EAP + ITSM + sprint deadline
"API migration at risk — blocking incident unresolved, sprint ends in 2 days."
Velocity + committed + history
"You'll complete 28 of 38 points. Here are 3 to descope to hit the goal."
Dependency + overnight change
"Dependency unblocked at 11pm. Pull that story into the sprint before standup."
Board activity + cycle time
"This item is stuck 3× longer than your team's average. Split or reassign?"

Each connects 2–3 systems. No standalone tool can produce them. That's not a dashboard — that's reasoning.

Act 05 — The vision

Collaboration that knows your enterprise.

CWM should stop competing with Asana and Monday on boards and docs — they'll always be good at that. CWM should compete on the one thing they can never build: collaboration surfaces that are aware of your ITSM incidents, your EAP sprints, your PPM approvals, and your HR requests — natively, without integrations.

Vision

CWM becomes the morning command center for SPM users — the one place you open first because it tells you what matters across every system, and why. Start with SPM teams. Prove the value. Then expand across ServiceNow.

North star

% of SPM users whose first app open of the day is CWM.

Three principles that guide every decision

Context, not data

AI knows your role, team, sprint, and priorities — not just your task list.

Value on arrival

The platform already knows you. First open shows your work — not an empty board.

Reason, don't display

"Sprint at risk because of an ITSM incident" is a feature. "Here's your ITSM data" is plumbing.

Act 06 — The product

Daily Pulse.

One product. Three questions. Every morning. Everything else CWM builds — sprint analytics, Connected Work, AI skills, search — feeds into this. They're not separate features. They're the engine behind the Pulse.

⚡

What matters right now

AI-ranked priorities with reasoning. "These 3 matter most — here's why." Each reason cites cross-system data the user couldn't see before.

△

What changed that affects your plan

Not "15 items moved state." Instead: "blocking incident resolved at 11pm — pull that story into the sprint before standup." Cause and effect across systems.

◎

What's at risk before it's too late

"Sprint at 120% of velocity. Dependency cascade from Team A to Team C. Item stuck 3× normal." Predictions — not reports.

Act 11 — The hard calls

Three objections this would face in its first review.

01
"Sprint analytics don't even exist yet. Why build AI on top?"
The tensionCWM doesn't have burndown charts or velocity. EAP has more mature sprint tools. Proposing intelligence before fixing foundations feels like putting the roof on before the walls.
Where I landThe roadmap addresses this. P1 ships sprint analytics before P3 ships cross-system reasoning. Foundation first. But the vision must be set now — otherwise P1 becomes "catch up to EAP" instead of "build toward something EAP can never do on its own."
02
"If nobody uses CWM now, why build a new surface on top of it?"
The tensionCWM adoption is still early. Building Daily Pulse means investing in a surface on top of an underused product. That's a bet on a bet.
Where I landThe Pulse IS the adoption mechanism. Today's onboarding fails because it asks users to build boards before seeing value. The Pulse inverts this — it shows value on day 1 using data that already exists. Users discover boards through Pulse insights, not through a template picker.
03
"Isn't this just a notification feed dressed up as intelligence?"
The tensionServiceNow already has notifications. Email already tells you when things change. A "what changed" feed risks becoming noise that users mute.
Where I landThe difference is reasoning. Email says "incident updated." The Pulse says "this incident blocks your sprint story, and your sprint ends Thursday — escalate now." The connection between systems is the value. If users mute it, we failed at reasoning quality, not the concept.
